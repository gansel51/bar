Manhattan

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Bourbon or Rye
  • 3/4 oz Sweet Vermouth
  • Angostura Bitters
  • Maraschino cherry

A bold, boozy mix of whiskey, sweet vermouth, and bitters. The Manhattan is a classic cocktail dating back to the late 1800s.

Whiskey Sour

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Bourbon
  • 3/4 oz Lemon juice
  • 3/4 oz Simple syrup
  • Egg white
  • Angostura bitters
  • Maraschino cherry

A refreshing blend of whiskey, lemon juice, and simple syrup. The egg white creates a silky texture and frothy top.

Old Fashioned

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Bourbon or Rye
  • 1/4 oz demerara syrup
  • Angostura bitters
  • Golden Bitters
  • Orange twist and lemon twist

A classic combination of whiskey, sugar, bitters, and an orange twist. One of the oldest known cocktails, dating back to the early 1800s.

Gin and Tonic

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Gin
  • 4 oz Tonic water
  • Lime wedge

A crisp and bubbly mix of gin and tonic water with lime. Originally created by British officers in India who mixed quinine tonic with gin to make it more palatable.

Tom Collins

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Gin
  • 1 oz Lemon juice
  • 3/4 oz Simple syrup
  • Club soda
  • Orange wedge and cherry

A refreshing mix of gin, lemon juice, and club soda. Named after a widespread practical joke that swept across New York in 1874.

Gin Fizz

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Gin
  • 1 oz Lemon juice
  • 3/4 oz Simple syrup
  • Egg white
  • Club soda

A frothy, refreshing classic featuring gin, citrus, and the silky texture of egg white. The Ramos Gin Fizz variation adds cream and orange flower water for extra decadence.

Negroni

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz Gin
  • 1 oz Campari
  • 1 oz Sweet vermouth
  • Orange twist

A bitter, complex mix of gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth. Legend has it that Count Camillo Negroni created it in Florence in 1919 when he asked for gin instead of soda in his Americano.

Gin Rickey

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Gin
  • 1 oz Lime juice
  • Club soda
  • Lime wedge

A refreshing mix of gin, lime juice, and club soda. Named after lobbyist Colonel Joe Rickey, who created the original version with bourbon in the 1880s.

Dark and Stormy

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Dark rum (preferably Gosling's Black Seal)
  • 4 oz Ginger beer
  • 1/2 oz Lime juice
  • Lime wedge

A spicy-sweet mix of dark rum and ginger beer with a splash of lime. The official drink of Bermuda, where Gosling's Black Seal rum originates.

Mojito

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Light rum
  • 3/4 oz Fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 oz Simple syrup
  • 6-8 Fresh mint leaves
  • Soda water
  • Mint sprig for garnish

A refreshing mix of light rum, mint, and lime juice. This classic Cuban cocktail has been popular since the early 1900s.

Daiquiri

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Light rum
  • 3/4 oz Fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 oz Simple syrup
  • Lime wheel for garnish

A classic mix of light rum, lime juice, and simple syrup. Named after a beach near Santiago, Cuba, where the drink was supposedly invented in the early 1900s.

Margarita

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Tequila
  • 1 oz Fresh lime juice
  • 3/4 oz Cointreau
  • Salt for rimming
  • Lime wheel for garnish

A tart, salty-sweet classic with tequila, lime juice, and orange liqueur. One of the most popular cocktails in the world, with dozens of origin stories dating to the 1930s and 40s.

Oaxacan Old Fashioned

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 oz Tequila Reposado
  • 1/2 oz Mezcal
  • 1/4 oz Agave syrup
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Flamed orange twist

Death & Co's famous twist on the classic, combining mezcal and tequila with agave syrup and bitters. Created by Phil Ward, it showcases the complementary flavors of tequila and mezcal.

Under the Volcano

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz Mezcal
  • 1 oz Amaro Nonnino
  • 1 oz Lusteau East India Sherry

A smoky, herbal cocktail from the legendary Hawthorne in Boston, MA. Named after Malcolm Lowry's novel, it balances the smoke of mezcal with amaro's herbal complexity and sherry's nutty richness.

Moscow Mule

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Vodka
  • 4 oz Ginger beer
  • 1/2 oz Fresh lime juice
  • Lime wedge for garnish

A zingy combo of vodka, ginger beer, and lime, typically served in a copper mug. Created in 1941 as a marketing collaboration to sell vodka and ginger beer in America.

  • Lemon twist or olive for garnish

A sophisticated mix of vodka and dry vermouth. While the gin martini is the original, the vodka version gained immense popularity in the mid-20th century.
